Gaussian distribution maximizes entropy for given covariance


Theorem 1

Let f:Rn→R be a continuous probability density function. Let X1,…,Xn be random variables with density f and with covariance matrix K, Ki⁢j=cov⁢(Xi,Xj). Let ϕ be the distribution of the multidimensional Gaussian (http://planetmath.org/JointNormalDistribution) with mean 0 and covariance matrix K. Then the Gaussian distribution maximizes the differential entropy for a given covariance matrix K. That is, h⁢(ϕ)≥h⁢(f).
